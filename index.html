Droplet coalescence

A physics-inspired visualization of droplets coalescence, run in the browser. The model is that of water vapor depositing on a horizontal planar surface. The rate of deposition is constant. Droplets move freely on the surface (this can be seen as a perfectly hydrophobic surface); when two droplets merge, the resulting droplet lies at the center of mass of the sources.

Note that the dynamics of droplet coalescence are surprisingly complex: there are known analytical solutions in the case of two droplets "Plane Stokes flow with a free boundary-driven solely by surface tension", and the more general case is an active field of research (see e.g. "Coalescence of bubbles and drops in an outer fluid"). In my model, coalescence is instantaneous.

The main challenge of the implementation is that a proper simulation requires a large number of samples (tens of thousands), which can pretty quickly become prohibitively expensive if implemented the naive way (n^3). This implementation uses a quadtree for acceleration and is in n*log(n).
